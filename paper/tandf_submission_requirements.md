## Article types

Only two article types are accepted:

| Type | Abstract | Word limit | Keywords |
|------|----------|------------|----------|
| **Research Article** | Unstructured, 200 words | Not specified | Not specified |
| **Data Note** | Structured, 250 words (Introduction / Methods / Results / Discussion) | 3000 words max | 3–6 |

---

## Peer review

**Single-anonymous** peer review by two independent expert reviewers.
Preprint servers: preprints are permitted but anonymity cannot be guaranteed; see T&F preprints policy.

---

## Manuscript structure

Both article types follow this order:

1. Title page
2. Abstract
3. Keywords
4. Main text: Introduction → Materials and Methods → Results → Discussion
5. Acknowledgements
6. Declaration of interest statement
7. References
8. Appendices (as appropriate)
9. Table(s) with caption(s) — each on individual pages
10. Figures
11. Figure captions (as a list)

---

## Style guidelines

- **Language:** English only; **British spelling** (-ise, not -ize) throughout
- **Quotation marks:** Double (`"..."`); single only for quotes within quotes (`"a 'quote' within"`)
- **Long quotations:** Indented, no quotation marks
- **Units:** SI units, non-italicized
- **Equations:** Must be editable (not images) if submitting in Word
- Refer to T&F quick style guidelines, not published articles

- Accepted formats: **Word** or **LaTeX**

## What to include: submission checklist

1. **Author details** — full name, affiliation (where research was conducted), email for corresponding author, ORCiD iDs, social media handles (optional). No post-acceptance affiliation changes.
2. **CRediT roles** — contributor roles taxonomy for all authors; will appear in published article.
3. **Funding statement** — format: *"This work was supported by [Agency] under Grant [number]."*
4. **Disclosure/conflict of interest statement** — use subheading "Disclosure of interest." If none: *"The authors report there are no competing interests to declare."*
5. **Data availability statement** — provide DOI, hyperlink, or persistent identifier for any associated dataset.
6. **Data deposition** — if sharing data openly, deposit in a recognized repository before or at submission; provide DOI or pre-reserved DOI.
7. **Supplemental material** — published via Figshare; provide a concise caption for each file.
8. **Figures** — separate high-resolution files (see resolution requirements above).
9. **Tables** — editable format.
10. **Equations** — editable in Word.
11. **Units** — SI throughout.
12. **Third-party permissions** — written permission required for any reproduced figures, tables, or text beyond fair use.
13. **Video abstract** (optional) — can be prepared through T&F Editing Services.

## Data sharing policy

Taylor & Francis Basic Data Sharing Policy applies. Authors are encouraged (not required) to:
- Deposit data in a recognized repository with a persistent identifier (DOI preferred)
- Cite datasets in the reference list
- Include a data availability statement
